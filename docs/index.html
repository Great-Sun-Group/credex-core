Credex Principle

If I owe you,
and you owe them,
and they owe me...
we're square.

The credex ecosystem actualizes the Credex Principle in the Minute Transaction Queue (MTQ), which tracks payable and receivable invoices for members' accounts, finds loops of value creation, and cancels payable and receivable invoices against each other in credloops in which every debt is reduced by an equal value. This is a GAAP-compliant accounting procedure that anyone and any business can benefit from, anywhere in the world.

In accounting terms, an outstanding credex is a contingent asset for one party and a contingent liability for the other. These asset/liability pairs are automatically strung together into credloops wherever possible across the credex ecosystem. When a credloop is found and cleared, every account in the loop will have an accounts payable invoice cleared against an equivalent accounts receivable invoice.

This behaviour enables members to replace the use of third party money or other debt instruments that are issued by an outside sovereign with accounting entries on a shared ledger that are backed only by the sovereign credit, assets, and value-producing capacities of the transacting members.

In so doing, we replace our reliance on the currencies of outside sovereigns and give ourselves options that insulate us from the financial dislocations of inflation and deflation in those currencies.

Credcoin Principle

I am entitled to receive one equal share
of the value our economy harvests from nature.

I am responsible to pay for the share
of natural wealth that I consume.

The credex ecosystem actualizes the Credcoin Principle for participating members in the Daily Credcoin Offering (DCO), in a process which anchors relative exchange rates across the ecosystem. Every 24h, the DCO makes this equation true: one credcoin today equals the number of members participating in the DCO today divided by the value of natural wealth entering the organic economy through the DCO today.

One credcoin is issued daily per DCO participant, each worth one equal share of the total value received in the DCO, which is paid in at market rates based on a participant's resource consumption relative to other participants. Those who consume more purchase that right fairly from those who consume less.

The organic economy is a subset of the credex ecosystem, which is a subset of the global economy, which is a human-enhanced subset of the universe that is given to us by nature. The organic economy is an economy in which wealth circulates, profit accumulates, investment is made, and gifts are given in alignment and long-term harmony with the biological environment of our habitat, the physical laws of our universe, and the spiritual laws of our existence.

The credex ecosystem stores values in credcoin (CXX). In order to natively enable multi-denominational accounting, a CXXmultiplier value is stored alongside every CXX value. Both of these values are updated in every DCO so that all accounting and reporting remains universally true both to the face value in the denomination specified, and to all other values stored in the ecosystem. A contract expresses it's CXX value when it needs a reference vs any other value in the ecosystem. A contract expresses it's face value when it needs a reference vs the original intentions of the counterparties. On-the-fly exchange rate calculations performed on CXX values enable any amount to be viewed or totaled in the current or past value of any denomination at any time.

The credex ecosystem uses the flows of value that are created by members participating in the DCO as the anchor point for exchange rates across the ecosystem. These flows, which can be as small as a single member, provide a mathematical peg that anchors financial reality in the credex ecosystem to the reality of underlying natural resource flows.

The multi-denominational mathematical mechanisms of the DCO manage the dynamic balance between:

  • Local truth: preserving the value of all contracts in their specified denomination.
  • Global truth: preserving the value of all contracts relative to every other value.
  • Past truth: preserving the value of past contracts relative to today's real resource flow values.
  • Future truth: preserving the value of all contracts independent of changing resource flow values.

Secured Credexes

The basic protocol underlying the credex ecosystem clears unsecured debts (invoices) against each other. Overlaying this protocol is the protocol for secured credexes. A secured credex balance is created when a member purchases a secured credex from an authorized and audited account such as the ones managed by VimbisoPay and Great Sun Financial.

Whereas unsecured credexes are based on pure peer-to-peer credit, accepting a secured credex provides the recipient assurances that the issuer has already provided an asset of real value to an audited third party that the recipient can now claim title to at any time (via credloop) simply by issuing an invoice to the securing party. Secured credexes provide onramp/offramp services for cash in and cash out of the ecosystem.

The credex ecosystem does not process currency, hold funds, or come into contact with money in any way. Secured credexes are not produced by the credex ecosystem. Credex is not money. Secured credexes exist in a one-to-one relationship with underlying assets held by third parties in audited bank accounts and vaults, where they securely and verifiably remain.

MyCredex.app provides moneyless accounting services, and as sovereign members we have come together to create a system that uses third-party verification to create accounting entries locked to real underlying assets.

Secured Credex Services

The credex ecosystem provides the non-sovereign and non-monetary services of:

  • Daily auditing of the trust accounts of secured credex agents like VimbisoPay and Great Sun Financial that are permitted to issue unrestricted secured credexes, and ensuring the amount issued matches the amount of currency held.
  • Only allowing unaudited accounts to issue secured credex in denominations and values that they have previously received into their account balances.
  • Isolating secured credexes of each denomination into their own substructure so that they only close in loops with other secured credexes of the same denomination, ensuring that the link from real assets to secured balances remains intact and auditable.
