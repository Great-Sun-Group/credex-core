Credcoin: Markets & Implementation

Gifts

Credcoin exists because humans want it to. Credcoin came into existence because a single human wanted it to, and it will exist as long as any single human wants it to. As long as the technological infrastructure exists, credcoin is an option that is open to us all.

The credcoin principle is an expression of sovereignty in which responsibility leads to belonging. Every day that you take a measure of personal responsibility for making good on your debt to all for the natural resources that you consume by voluntarily participating in the gift portion of the DCO, you receive your personal birthright of 1.000 CXX. Every day that at least one human participates in the DCO, the value of credcoin can be determined, and the DCO will continue to anchor the credex ecosystem.

The power of gifts

In a world where the organic economy thrives at scale, your gift to the DCO is a relatively small portion of the total value that you contribute. In a world where the organic economy is the only economy, your DCO gift is pure charity, gifted to all on a sliding scale where the greatest positive impact is on those whose resource consumption is lowest.

In our world today, in which the organic economy is only starting to be recognized and cultivated, your DCO gift is what powers the heartbeat of organic economic circulation. Your gift is what makes the organic economy real. Your gift brings your consumption into the measured flows of the fully manifest and measurable organic economy. Your gift enables sovereignty, freedom, sustainability, mutual wealth creation, and economic justice, and supports the existence of the credex ecosystem. The amount of your gift can be small, but the leveraged impact will be massive.

Gift mechanics

Credcoin exists because humans want to make good on their debt to all for their consumption of natural resources. The most direct path to act on this desire is to give in the DCO.

Your DCO gift is given in currency, of any denomination. These gifts are pooled together and held, and promissory notes are issued back to each DCO participant for one equal share of the total value pooled. These promissory notes are contracts for 1.000 CXX. In a world where the organic economy is just emerging into consciousness and the gift DCO is the backbone of credcoin, one credcoin is equal to one equal share of the currency that has been pooled and held. In this newly forming organic economy, pools of currency held in audited accounts back the 1.000 CXX contracts that are issued to each participant.

Gift levels

Each participant sets their own level of gift in credcoin. Until the market DCO is implemented, a gift amount over 1.000 CXX indicates acknowledgement of consumption of more than one equal share of natural resources. This level of gift is appropriate for the global wealthy, including most of us in North America, Europe, Japan, modernized parts of China, many global cities, and other regions and localities where wealth and capital has accumulated.

I participate in the gift DCO at greater than 1.000 CXX out of:

  • Loyalty, obligation and duty. I am loyal towards my fellow sovereign humans. The resources that sustain me come from around the world, and the right to harvest them ultimately belongs to all of us as sovereigns. I give to express my loyalty to this sovereignty.
  • Charity and wanting to truly help those less economically fortunate. I give because I want to raise the economic floor for basic human existence across the Earth.
  • Respect for freedom and choice. The DCO and the empowerments and responsibilities it embodies are fundamentally non-coercive. I give because I want to embed the values of human sovereignty into the core of our global social contract.
  • The sustainability imperative. I give because I want to ground our economy and our wealth in the bounty of the biosphere, instead of its destruction.
  • Desire for systemic change built on human-centric and earth-centric foundations. I give because I want to throw my weight behind this momentum towards positive systemic transformation.
  • Belief that the market DCO will be implemented. Price-based signals must be embedded into the organic economy in order to align individual human freedom with the global imperative for sustainability. I give to lay down a pathway that market-based solutions can follow.
  • Desire to bring my personal resource consumption into the organic economy, starting in a small, simple, and low-risk way.

Full-cost accounting

The market DCO incorporates full-cost accounting into prices that participating businesses charge and participating members pay. The purpose of the market DCO is to incorporate the full ecological costs of production into market prices at every stage of the economic process. This, but only this, is required for the organic economy to maintain harmony with the biosphere. Your participation in the DCO starts to bring your consumption into the organic economy, where it can exist in symbiotic resonance with our natural world.

Renewable resources are harvested by the organic economy at a level that can be sustained indefinitely. Non-renewable resources are harvested at a level and utilized in a manner that respects future generations. All wastes released are feedstock for biological or naturally-occurring chemical processes, at a level that does not have a negative systemic impact on our overall habitat over any time scale, and that minimizes local environmental degradations.

Symbiotic relationship

Working in a symbiotic relationship, the organic economy is formed by its environment, and forms its environment, in mutually beneficial ways. Wastes exiting the organic economy sustain certain streams of natural wealth, and natural wealth that has economic value is harvested at environmentally sustainable levels.

The relationship that the organic economy has with its natural environment sets the constraints within which it operates. Bringing our consumption within these constraints, and therefore within the organic economy, opens the door to endless prosperity. Consuming outside the organic economy—outside the constraints of sustainability—leads inevitably towards collective impoverishment.

Market mechanisms

The sustainably-harvested natural wealth entering the organic economy has specific economic value that can be determined via market mechanisms. By incorporating this value into organic economy prices, we harmonize with the biosphere. By dividing the value equally among all participating sovereign humans, we respect the right to one equal share. By paying organic economy prices, we fulfill our responsibility to provide value to all for our consumption of resources, and we move our consumption towards balance with our habitat.

Organic economy accounting is full-cost accounting, which integrates the true cost of natural resources and wastes into the prices of all products and services.

Certification over regulation

The natural wealth entering the organic economy could in theory be regulated, and that might be our first instinct. But instead of being regulated, it is certified.

Regulation requires broad agreement, compliance, and enforcement, and is fundamentally coercive. The regulation-based approach leads to campaigning for all-or-nothing victory, implementation at scale, and enforced compliance. The regulation-based approach puts implementation off into the future and leaves our entire vision in the hands of forces beyond our control, which must be overcome before we can live our values.

Certification is nimble, can be applied at different levels and on different criteria as needed, and is fundamentally about creating informed choice rather than compliance. Certification is implemented in the ecosystem with visual badges and codes that are linked to data that proves compliance with the criteria of any kind of certification.

Making a market and a democracy

Our starting place is that the natural wealth entering the organic economy belongs equally to all. Decisions must be made to define the sustainable harvest of natural resources across all domains. Millions and billions of decisions must be made, and remade, at global and local scales, across short and vast timelines, to define and redefine sustainable harvests.

This is a non-negotiable requirement for a sustainable economy and society. Sustainability can't remain a vague idea, it has to be real and specific in every context, and it has to change as reality changes or our understanding grows. It takes work.

We are focused on the non-negotiables here, and we must remain open to the fact that reasonable people can and will disagree not only about what sustainable harvests are, but also about who gets to decide. Many different possibilities are imaginable. However, if we truly want to extend sovereignty into the political realm, we must operate from the premise that democratic processes that prize equality of voice are the appropriate tools for these decisions. One sovereign human equals one sovereign vote in these matters.

From politics to business

From the political sphere, where democracy holds sway, emerges the right to harvest a specific natural resource, in a specific quantity, from a specific geography. This right forms the backbone of the market DCO. This is where business and personal ownership take over and the principles of value, markets, and capital hold sway.

Business takes over from politics by purchasing the politically-granted rights to harvest specific natural resources, from a specific location, within a specific timeframe, under specific conditions. Through the DCO the revenue from this sale is distributed equally amongst all participating humans.

If a hundred fish can sustainably be harvested out of a specific lake in a specific month, those rights can be auctioned to fishers. They would likely be individuals, whose families would feast.

If fifty tonnes of fish can be sustainably harvested from an ocean ecosystem in a specific month, those rights can be auctioned to fishers. They would likely be corporate entities that could mobilize resources and economic capital to create efficiencies at this scale. Within the bounds of sovereignty and sustainability established in the DCO, this corporate efficiency that brings lower prices should be celebrated.

Capital that accumulates through serving human needs expressed through free-market choices, in harmony with the biosphere and respecting sovereignty, is to be celebrated as the highest economic good, matched only by the gifting enabled by such riches.

Getting real

Through the DCO we take personal responsibility for bringing our own consumption into the organic economy in a simple and direct way, exercising our rights and responsibilities without demanding anything of anyone else.

We don't need international treaties, broad agreements, and full compliance for us to start to bring our own personal consumption into the organic economy. We are habituated to think that we need someone in charge to fix things. We need to work together, but we cannot let the actions or inactions of others cause us to abdicate our personal responsibility to our Earth and our fellow humans.

Badge-based implementation

Natural resources can enter the organic economy at any point in their life cycle. Human consumption in one form or another drives all economic activity. We do not need to fight political wars to force multinational fishing companies to buy rights to fisheries, rights that they have in fact already been granted by existing regulations. That approach, and that entire way of thinking, leads to disempowered dead ends.

A fish can enter the organic economy from my kitchen, from the local retail fish market, the delivery truck, the wharf, the boat, or anywhere else along the value creation chain, all the way back to the moment it becomes a permissibly harvestable resource.

With a badge-based approach, an organic economy badge can be purchased at any point along the chain, and from that moment until consumption, the resource exists within the organic economy. A retail market could buy already certified organic economy fish, or could buy uncertified fish and purchase the badges to certify them; either way the result is the same. The costs of purchasing the badge will be passed on to the consumer, and the proceeds will be distributed equally in the DCO.

Prototyping

The badge-based approach is not intended to replace regulation-based limits and protections on natural resources. It is intended to demonstrate that we can use limits themselves to form a reality-connected economic and financial bedrock capable of supporting a thriving organic economy.

The badge-based approach enables us to take slow and simple steps towards our goal, without demanding anything from anyone else. Regulations are ultimately required to ensure sustainability and fairness, but the far greater need is for economic models that work in cooperation with our environment. We need to experiment and build, not fight political wars over regulation. Especially when no one really has all the answers yet anyways. We've got a lot to figure out together, and if we step down the hubris and righteousness we'll drastically increase our personal and collective capacity to get shit done.

The sovereignty-empowering, badge-based approach opens a path that we can proceed along based on free personal choice. We can give ourselves permission to start small and low risk, even to use wildly approximate values, to innovate, to play and experiment. Our attitude could be… This credcoin idea and implementation is pretty neat, what if it works? What if it could scale? What if this is the start of something completely different?

We don't make the credcoin principle real by forcing it, we make it real by prototyping it.
