Due Diligence

Mathematical logic and conceptual specifics

Real World Outcomes

1. Organizing and incentivising the distributed production of economic value

The credex ecosystem enables low friction value creating exchange of real goods and services.

2. Building economic resilience because modern money is systemically vulnerable to existing and looming changes in its environment

The credex ecosystem enables transactions independent of modern money and money systems, yet integrated seamlessly with them.

3. Financially aligning an economic network to produce maximum value within evolving limits of sustainability

The credex ecosystem and the daily credcoin offering will bring market dynamics and the full power of capital to bear on the challenge of aligning an economic network within the limits of sustainability.

4. Building widespread multigenerational wealth

  • The credex ecosystem enables widespread accumulation of real and financial capital across a network of economic nodes.
  • The credex ecosystem enables low friction transactions and value creating loops of exchange to build this capital.
  • The credex ecosystem is a financial network that can stably operate within the limits of ecological sustainability, and therefore endure for the long term.

5. Empowering human sovereignty

  • The credex ecosystem empowers all of its members with the daily right to consume natural resources at sustainable levels, and provides a voluntary and business friendly mechanism to implement this in a way that moves our economic network towards wealth generating alignment with the biosphere.
  • The credex ecosystem empowers all members to create their own medium of exchange, and control its value by the provision (or lack thereof) of real value as contracted.
  • The credex ecosystem offers all of its members the power to own their own data and share it on their own terms only. (This right is currently enforced only programmatically, with cryptographic enforcement to be implemented with the future development of web3 infrastructure.)

FIG. 7 illustrates an example DCO Day 6 - Day 7

The DCO is self-sustaining, even without currency (or some other asset) involved, however the ecosystem would lose the ability to reliably denominate credexes in anything other than CXX.

FIG. 8 illustrates an example DCO Security Account

Funds will be released from the DCO security account when the DCO intermediary (the holder of the account) receives a specific type of credex, coded (via smart contract or DCO intermediary activity) to transfer money to the counterparty only when the credex is credlooped through the Credex Foundation. The intermediary's debt to the Credex Foundation is retired as currency is released.

A premium will be charged when seeking to buy currency with credex. That premium will be set at 2% on launch, and will be market driven once there are other DCO intermediaries to create a market between them.

If the value of the natural wealth entering the organic economy changes significantly from one day to the next, as in this scenario, credex denominated in CXX will numerically fluctuate in lockstep with changes in that flow, while the value will remain rock steady.

Week 1, according to Alice

On Day01 Alice gives 1.000 CXX, indicating one equal share of natural resources consumption for her that day. On Day01, 1.000 CXX is equivalent to $1.00 CAD. She gives $1.00 (not shown above) and receives a credex worth $1.00 CAD (DCO-R01) as Day02 begins.

From Day02 through Day05, Alice has her DCO gift amount set at 1.010, indicating consumption of 1% more than one equal share of natural resources. On Day02, 1.010 CXX is equivalent to $1.01 CAD. She gives $1.01 and receives a credex worth $1.01 CAD (DCO-R02) as Day03 begins.

On Day03, 1.010 CXX is equivalent to $1.02 CAD. She gives $1.02 and receives a credex worth $1.22 CAD (DCO-R03) as Day04 begins. This is because Bob joined, giving 1.400 CXX, indicating greater relative resource consumption, whose value is shared among all DCO participants (including Bob himself).

On Day04, 1.010 CXX is equivalent to $1.23 CAD. She gives $1.23 and receives a credex worth $1.23 CAD (DCO-R05) as Day05 begins. Charlie has joined at 0.550 CXX, and the change in flow of one equal share is so minor today that it becomes a rounding error in CAD. The wealth that previously flowed from Bob to Alice now flows to Charlie due to his lower consumption of natural resources.

On Day05, 1.010 CXX is equivalent to $1.24 CAD. She gives $1.24 and receives a credex worth $1.27 CAD (DCO-R08) as Day06 begins.

On Day06 when she views her DCO receive transactions in CAD, she sees the total amount of $5.72 CAD. When she views her account in CXX, she sees a total of 4.519 times the value of the natural wealth entering the organic economy on Day06.
