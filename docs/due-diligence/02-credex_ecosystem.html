Mathematical logic and conceptual specifics

The Credex Ecosystem

What is the credex ecosystem?

A credex is a payment by smart contract. If I owe you and you owe me, we can offset our debts against each other and cancel them without money changing hands. If I owe you, you owe Alice, and Alice owes me, we can offset these debts against each other and cancel them, without money changing hands.

The credex ecosystem implements this principle at scale seamlessly across all currency denominations, asset classes, and derivatives using low friction smart contracts to enable moneyless payments, commerce, and finance.

The credex ecosystem solves the problems identified:

  1. Organizing and incentivising the distributed production of economic value
  2. Building economic resilience because modern money is systemically vulnerable to existing and looming changes in its environment
  3. Financially aligning an economic network to produce maximum value within evolving limits of sustainability
  4. Building widespread multigenerational wealth
  5. Empowering human sovereignty

The credex ecosystem is a digital space that enables:

  • Transaction contracts anchored to government fiat, denominated in currency.
  • Transaction contracts anchored to the value of specific assets, denominated in anything with a verifiable market price (commodity, bond, equity, etc).
  • Transaction contracts anchored to any formula based value structure, derivative, combination, average, conditional statement, etc. The credex ecosystem puts the full flexibility and power of programmatic smart contracts at the fingertips of every producer, trader, investor and consumer, with freedom for developers and service providers to build their own smart contracts within the rules of the ecosystem and link them to digital, financial, and real assets (protocols under development).
  • Transaction contracts anchored to the real wealth entering the organic economy today, denominated in CXX.
  • Secure web3 digital identity management, with native permission structures for storing and securely sharing data of any kind (protocols under development).
  • Opportunity for voluntary participation in the daily credcoin offering (DCO) to help us orient the production, exchange and consumption of value within the network towards sustainability and mutual sovereignty.

Like money, credex scales the barter-based principle of value creating exchange across time and space, and to multiple participants. Unlike money, the credex ecosystem achieves this result without leverage or abstraction. Credex preserves the strengths of barter and minimizes its weaknesses, and credex also preserves the strengths of money and minimizes its weaknesses.

Smart contract

A credex is a smart contract for one member to provide a specified amount of value to another member, within a specified amount of time. Expressed in English, the credex contract template is:

<member1> promises to provide <member2> with <amount/formula> <denomination/formula>
worth of value by <date>.

Examples

Alice promises to provide Bob with $10.00 CAD
worth of value by June 22, 2023.

Alice promises to provide Bob with 0.001 BTC
worth of value by June 22, 2023.

Alice promises to provide Bob with 0.022 XAU
worth of value by June 22, 2023.

Alice promises to provide Bob with 1 share of Acme Inc.
worth of value by June 22, 2023.

Alice promises to provide Bob with the difference in value
between 1 barrel of Brent crude and $100 USD on market close on June 22, 2023 
worth of value by June 25, 2023.

These smart contracts replace money as a placeholder for value received but not yet provided, and provided but not yet received. They replace money completely, because even if a credex is denominated in CAD, no Canadian dollars are ever required to fulfill the contract, and the contract is not enforceable by the provision of CAD, except at the discretion of the acceptor of the credex. The promise is to provide a specific measure of value. The contract is specifically for the value, not the money which measures it.

The ecosystem in action

If Alice provides Bob with a widget, Bob provides Charlie a blidget, and Charlie provides Alice a tridget, each worth $10, and they pay each other with credex smart contracts, the contracts will find this loop when the last transaction is completed, and will mark each contract as fully redeemed.

In this way, value is created for all participants in a credloop. They each get what they want. Each credex will be part of multiple credloops until it is fully redeemed. Credexes are automatically redeemed across denominations (and asset classes, derivatives and formulas) with daily market rates.

Leverage of existing assets

A credex can be integrated with money or other assets, effectively leveraging them into additional value. This puts the same leveraged-based money creation impulse used by banks and shadow banks in the modern money system at the fingertips of every member, while keeping any default risks localized to that member and their counterparties. Such smart contracts might look like: 

Alice promises to provide Bob with $10.00 CAD
worth of value by June 25, 2023
and any portion of this value not provided by
midnight UTC on the above date will be
debited from her CAD security account and
credited to Bob's.

Alice promises to provide Bob with the difference in value in CAD
between 1 barrel of Brent crude and $100 USD on market close on June 22, 2023
worth of value by June 25, 2023
and 63.25% of any of this value not provided by midnight UTC on
the above date will be sold from her US1Y (US Treasuries)
security account and the proceeds deposited to Bob's EUR account.
(the purpose here being one way of specifically and easily sharing a specific amount of default risk and enabling a specific amount of leverage between counterparties)

Credex vs money

The credex ecosystem alleviates our need for money by offering an alternate medium of exchange: specific value creating smart contracts, between specific economic participants, linked together in specific credloops.

Unlike money, credex does not have a limited supply, is highly decentralized, has high degrees of transparency while maintaining privacy, empowers the sovereignty of every member, is created to serve the counterparties involved directly rather than for the profit of a financial institution, provides near zero friction payment processing across all currency denominations, financial asset classes, commodities, cryptocurrencies, derivatives and formulas, is concrete and specific rather than abstract, and is far more adaptable to a full world. Credex is like money, but better.

Where money is fungible and abstract, credexes are non-transferable and specific. A credex is a non-transferable smart contract between two economic participants. When I receive a credex, I can't spend it. I issue a new credex every time I spend, and accept a new credex every time I get paid. The credex ecosystem clears my expenses against my income in credloops of mutual value exchange.

Money will purchase the same value for anyone who holds it, credex will not. An outstanding credex holds value only to the economic participant who accepted it, and its value is entirely dependent on the economic participant who issued it and the paths that can be found to provide the value from the issuer to the acceptor. A credex is a placeholder that only holds potential value to a specific acceptor node until that value has been provided to the acceptor in actuality by that specific issuer node via one or more specific credloops.

Abstraction VS trusted specificity

A credex is a promise to provide value to a counterparty in the future. An outstanding credex represents an imbalance between value provided/promised and value received. A redeemed credex represents a balance between value promised and value received.

Every outstanding credex has an embedded default risk associated with a specific economic participant. Therefore, even when two credexes have the same face value, the unique credit risk associated with each credex and the inability of participants to exchange or transfer credexes differentiates credex from money.

The specificity of credex differentiates it from money even further. Even if two credexes have the same face value, and were issued and redeemed on the same days so no longer bear default risk, they are still not equivalent like the money version of that transaction would be. With credex, an economic participant can see which payables were cleared against which receivables, so can know specifically the multiple values that were provided and received in every completed transaction. Money as a placeholder endures in a way that disconnects it from its origin (which for modern money is in the capital leveraging process) and therefore money can only give us an abstract and time-limited currency equivalence of goods and services. Credex is different because it only holds a place (debt/credit) until the specific values in the contract have been delivered, and from that moment exists only as a record of that exchange of specific values (goods/services) and the way that value was delivered/redeemed along specific credloop paths.

If the issuer of a credex fails to provide the contracted value to the acceptor, that default is tracked, and can be compiled into credit scores (with various degrees of cryptographically secured privacy and transparency to fit every situation) that future counterparties will surely request and require before transacting. If a member does not fulfill their promises, future counterparties will be cautious to transact. In this way credit risk is localized and becomes purely between nodes, with no need for central oversight or authorization.

With these mechanisms, the credex ecosystem personalizes what the money economy abstracts. Credexes are issued and tracked in a distributed manner at the exact moments of value creating exchange. Credex is created as nodes take the specific risks they are comfortable to take in order to build mutual wealth in cooperation with specific counterparties. Counterparties are enabled to transact with each other frictionlessly to the degree that they trust each other, and tools such as credit ratings and security accounts are provided to help increase that mutual trust. A proliferation of these tools will be developed as the system evolves and matures.

Default risks are kept local between counterparties, and are always limited only to outstanding credex balances. Redeemed credexes are a record of value already provided and received, and carry no default risk. The successful credex ecosystem will blaze a trail of value created across time and space for all participants. New outstanding balances will constantly be created, and these imbalances will lead the way toward mutual value creation, which will become realized one specific exchange at a time as the economy flows forward.

Limited supply VS unlimited availability

Money supply must be limited. Money is required for transactions, so limited money reduces opportunities for value creating exchange. Credex can be frictionlessly created anywhere and anytime that two economic participants trust in their ability to create mutual value.

Risk of systemic bankruptcy or currency destruction VS effortless credex balance

A money economy is always balancing somewhere on the scale of inflation to deflation. Spiraling deflation (lack of money) leads to systemic bankruptcy and spiraling inflation (excess of money) leads to currency destruction. These are simply non-issues in the credex ecosystem, where money supply cannot be a concern, because there is no money.

Centralized and opaque VS decentralized, transparent, and secure

Modern money requires hierarchical chains of authorizations and permissions cascading outwards from central money-creating authorities. Credex requires a single (member owned and not-for-profit) accounting intermediary to implement and manage a decentralized, transparent, auditable, privacy securing, individually empowering, contract creation and fulfillment process.

Created for third party profit VS created for first party value

Money is created by authorized and permitted institutions for the purpose of generating a profit. Credex is created by transacting members for the purpose of enabling a specific value creating exchange, and no third party extracts profit from the transaction. The risks are borne directly by the counterparties who are cooperating to create value. The motive to create credex is value first, profit second. With money it's profit first, value second.

Payment processing

Payment processing on the distributed ecosystem ledger opens the full payment and value tracking process to auditable innovation, automation, and simplification. Client apps can process payments with a few taps, and payment automations can be built into digital processes through API calls and other modern programming tools to streamline the tracking of payments and the value creation process across economic chains with incredible specificity, precision, and automation.

Money creation, interest and capital deployment

Credex is created when value is created. Money is created when capital is leveraged.

Value creation and circulation is the primary economic activity. Capital accumulation occurs when circulating value collects with specific nodes in an economic network. Capital growth occurs when accumulated capital is invested in value producing economic activity. Value creation is the activity from which capital creation naturally arises. Value creation is highly distributed across an economic network, while capital creation occurs only in certain nodes in an economic network.

In the credex ecosystem, as in any economic network with free exchange, capital will accumulate with certain nodes. This capital needs a growth outlet in the form of investments. Smart contracts that invest or loan accumulated capital behave differently in the credex ecosystem than transaction contracts that are associated directly with value creating exchange. These investment protocols are still under development and there will be many options to explore and implement (first modelled on equities, bonds, loans, derivatives, etc, and growing from there) but a key feature will be the provision of interest and return on investment.

Credex is created in a distributed manner without interest when value is created. All credexes are temporary and expiring placeholders that play a critical role in the ongoing free flow of the production and circulation of goods and services of real value, and the accumulation, investment, and growth of capital. The credex ecosystem offers customizable smart contracts for each stage of the financial process that can be directly linked with every stage of globally distributed or hyperlocal production processes. The ecosystem incorporates all currency denominations, asset classes, specific real and financial assets, derivatives and formulas, and most importantly the ongoing flows of value creation that sustain real wealth.

In the money economies, money is created with interest when capital is leveraged. Created money in a sense disappears when the associated balance sheet liability is cleared, but the interest charged on that created money is never reversed out of the money system, and the assets are almost always quickly re-leveraged. This creates a constant debt overhang which propels the money economy forward. This propulsion embeds a potent bias for growth into money economies. These economies have evolutionarily optimized around this engine, which powered money effectively and powerfully in an empty world. Money excels and outcompetes in an empty world because of the growth impulse embedded in this overhang.

However, a money economy cannot enable a truly free flow of value creation and circulation, because its embedded growth bias is a constraint on the network as a whole that influences the behaviour of nodes in the specific direction of individual competition for resources/money to fuel their financial growth. Competition and cooperation will always both be present in systems of free exchange. Systemic emphasis on competition-for-growth has been proven to succeed in an empty world. We believe that systemic emphasis on cooperation-for-preservation will work better in a full world, and money works against this purpose.

Money is a scarce resource that must be competed for. Due to the debt overhang there is never quite enough money to fulfill all obligations, which puts constant pressure to compete for the scarce resource of money, which is the only thing that can be used to repay the portion of the debt overhang that is borne by each node in the network.

The irony is that in a full world, a strategy of competition for capital growth is likely to be less successful than one of cooperation for the preservation of wealth generating assets and processes. It is the requirement for competitive growth that is hardwired into the debt overhang of money economies that may have been their greatest strength, and may be becoming their greatest weakness. The unfulfillable demand for growth is at the root of the weakness that leads to the instability that is building up in system-critical nodes of the money network.

Capital follows real growth, wherever it leads. In a full world, money economies are poised to fail at delivering a rich growth environment for capital, and this failure of growth may ironically be due to their unwavering bias towards growth at all costs.

The credex ecosystem offers a better, more accountable, freer, more data rich and customizable, lower friction environment for capital growth than money economies can offer. In a full world, we believe that capital deployed in the credex ecosystem will be more effective and create greater real returns than capital deployed in the growth dependent money economies.

At the very deepest level, credex is more aligned to the purpose of organizing and incentivising the distributed production and circulation of economic value. Money creation is driven by capital growth and therefore requires it. Credex creation is driven by value creation, and therefore requires it. This tunes the ecosystem firstly to the needs of distributed value creation, and secondly to capital accumulation and growth. The credex ecosystem aligns with actual value creation processes and so it fades to the background as it enables the creation, circulation, and accumulation of real values, and the investment of accumulated capital. Credex freely offers and empowers growth in any direction, which is the environment in which capital thrives. Money requires and demands competitive growth, focused on obtaining the scarce resource of money itself.

Demurrage

Demurrage, as outlined by Gesell and Steiner, is the principle of the depreciation of money over time.

This principle is embodied in credex in a more specific way than is possible with money. Every credex must expire and lose its power as a medium of exchange. If the value promised is not delivered, this default risk must be realized and cleared off the balance sheets of affected nodes. Were this not the case, these risks would be systemized with similar dynamics and results as occur in the money economy.

A credex, once issued, will either be fulfilled and fully redeemed by a specific date, or any remaining balance will default on that date. In the credex ecosystem, this default is known as demurrage. The specificity of credex allows us to take demurrage to mean the consumption of the units of the medium of exchange within a purely financial context, disassociated from the specific consumption of value within the real economy. When a credex is redeemed, the real value it represents has been promised, provided and consumed, and the credex loses its active power as a medium of exchange. When a credex is demurrages, a portion of the value it promises is never provided or consumed, and it loses its active power as a medium of exchange. In this context, actualized demurrage is a negative for both participants involved and for the system as a whole, but the possibility of demurrage is a necessary and systemically positive force. It is in the interests of every node and the system as a whole to prevent demurrage, but demurrage localizes credit risk, and so it must exist as a possibility. Elimination of risk would disconnect finance from the underlying economic reality, which would be against the interests of all nodes.

Gift and consumption

Capital accumulates around assets and processes that create and distribute economic value. These processes are usually embodied in successful, profit producing businesses connected with each other up, down, and across multiple value creating flows. Successful investment of the capital that accumulates around these clusters of nodes moves the value created there elsewhere in the economic network. The purpose of this movement is to generate value and capital where it is invested, and also in the form of returns to the investing node. Value creation enables capital growth, which enables value creation and more capital growth in a positive feedback dynamic.

The credex ecosystem recognizes and empowers the gift alongside the purchase. A gift credex might behave exactly like a purchase credex, but is nevertheless identified as a gift because the gift plays a critical and recognized role within the credex ecosystem.

A full purchase process in the ecosystem includes these steps:

  1. Value is provided by one node to another
  2. A credex is issued and accepted (as a promise to provide reciprocal value)
  3. The promised value is provided via credloops and the credex is redeemed

A full gift process in the ecosystem includes these steps:

  1. Value is generated in production and purchase and accumulates with a node
  2. A credex is issued and accepted (as a promise to gift accumulated value)
  3. The promised value is provided via credloops and the credex is redeemed

As critical as value creating purchases are for wealth and wellbeing, the gift is still the primal solution to this problem. A well directed gift can be even more potent for value creation than a purchase. Charity that changes the circumstances of a creative human and allows them to reach their full potential can easily provide the greatest return for the wealth and wellbeing of all. This potential systemic return is matched only perhaps by gifts that increase the natural capital of our earth in a way that will impact generations to come. In both cases, the fact that these are gifts without expected return is critical, and it is recognized and celebrated within the ecosystem.

Just as capital naturally accumulates around value creating processes, gifts naturally accumulate around capital. Recognizing and celebrating this tendency within the ecosystem will open up entirely new channels for the systemic generation of wealth and wellbeing via altruistic gifts, charity, and "investing" in the whole rather than the parts, with no expectation of personal return.

In the credex ecosystem, a gift is seen as a purely consumptive activity from an economic perspective. In that capacity, the gift has the power to counteract the self-referential buildup of capital in a way that releases and dampens its potentially dangerous power if left unchecked. At the same time as being systemically beneficial, it is deeply humanly fulfilling to be able to give and be recognized and appreciated for it. The invisible hand of generosity is equally as potent as the invisible hand of the market, and equally important for a complete and integrated view of economic activity. Credex holds this integrated view, and is able to customize individual smart contracts for each step in the process. Money simply cannot compete.

Rudolph Steiner must again be noted here for his contributions theoretically distinguishing between the functions and behaviours of transaction money, loan/investment money, and gift money. Credex is able to implement these principles (and others that already exist or are yet to be created) with a specificity and level of sophistication that has never before been possible.

The distributed nature of credex will allow ideas and implementations to be developed, tested in simulation, prototyped in isolated pockets, and scaled. Credex vastly expands the horizons of possibility for financial innovation on a technical level and on the level of first principles, is unmatched in its ability to test, simulate and provide granular level reporting on performance, while also protecting privacy in transparent, user controlled ways that can be negotiable to various degrees within each credex depending on the protocols for the type of contract being designed and the behaviours enabled by the ecosystem.
